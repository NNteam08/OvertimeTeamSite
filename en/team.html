Team Overtime

Our name and philosophy

"Overtime" is more than a name — it is our philosophy. In sports, overtime means extra time when the outcome of the match is decided. In FTC it is the moment when every second and every decision matters.

We believe in the concept of "clutch" — the ability to perform at peak pressure when everything is on the line. It is not only about competition but about life: finding solutions in critical situations, working until the last second and never giving up.

Our team is built on the principles of resilience, dedication and the willingness to push on when others stop. We don't just build robots — we build character and prepare for the challenges of the future.

Our Story

Team Overtime is a team competing in FIRST Tech Challenge for the first time in the 2025-2026 season. However, our journey in robotics started long before that. We actively participated in various FIRST programs, including FIRST LEGO League (FLL) and FIRST Global Challenge (FGC), as well as other robotics competitions.

Our experience across numerous robotics competitions has given us a deep understanding of engineering principles and strong skills in programming, design and teamwork. We learned from every challenge, constantly improving our abilities and developing innovative approaches to solving problems.

Now that we have joined FIRST Tech Challenge, we apply all of this accumulated experience to reach new heights. We are devoted to outreach and education, mentoring new teams, organizing workshops and sharing our knowledge with the robotics community in Kazakhstan.

Our goal is to make robotics accessible to every student, regardless of background, and to inspire the next generation of engineers and innovators through participation in FIRST Tech Challenge.

Our Contribution to FIRST

Team Overtime works actively to grow the FIRST community in Kazakhstan — building infrastructure for new teams and sharing knowledge with beginning roboticists.

🌐

Educational Website

We created and maintain a comprehensive educational resource with learning materials, tutorials and guides for teams at every level. The site is available in multiple languages and helps newcomers get started quickly with FTC.

🎓

Mentorship and Training

We regularly run workshops and online sessions for new teams. Over the last two seasons we helped launch 2 new teams that continue to successfully participate in competitions.

🌍

Connections with Teams Abroad

We establish and maintain partnerships with teams from different countries, exchange experience, knowledge and best practices. We participate in international initiatives and collaborative projects to grow the global FIRST community.

📢

Screamage

We organized and ran Screamage — a large-scale event for the robotics community bringing together teams, mentors and enthusiasts. The event contributed to experience sharing and strengthened connections within the community.

📖

FTC Books and Guide for the Blind

We developed and published special materials and FTC guides adapted for people with visual impairments. Includes audio versions, tactile diagrams and special formats to make robotics accessible to everyone.

🌟

Openness and Accessibility

All of our materials, code, designs and knowledge are open to the community. We believe that the growth of FIRST in Kazakhstan depends on the joint efforts of all teams.

Our Mission

To build a sustainable infrastructure for the growth of FIRST in Kazakhstan, so that every student — regardless of location or resources — can take part in robotics competitions and develop their STEM skills.

Our Projects & Technical Achievements

Highlights of our robot subsystems and technical innovations from past seasons.

⚙️

Custom Drivetrain

Designed and built a high-performance mecanum drivetrain with optimized gear ratios for maximum speed and maneuverability. Achieved 95% efficiency in field testing.

🎯

Precision Intake System

Developed a dual-roller intake mechanism with sensor feedback for reliable pickup of game elements. The system handles multiple object types with a 98% success rate.

🤖

Advanced Autonomous

Implemented odometry-based localization with AprilTag detection for precise positioning. Autonomous routines achieve consistent scoring in competition matches.

📐

CAD & Design

A full CAD design of the robot with detailed assemblies and manufacturing drawings. All custom parts are engineered for manufacturability and reliability.

💻

Software Architecture

Modular software framework with reusable subsystems, state machines and comprehensive telemetry. The codebase follows best practices for maintainability.

🔧

Manufacturing Excellence

In-house manufacturing capabilities including 3D printing, CNC machining and precision assembly. Quality control processes ensure reliable robot performance.
